01 The Problem

Elite brokers don't have time to waste on the phone.

The LAAA Team has closed 455 transactions worth $1.45 billion. That volume is built by ten specialists, each carrying their own pipeline across seven LA submarkets. Multiply that across the whole team and the opportunity cost becomes significant.

Ten specialists. Seven submarkets. Every one of them unavailable at some point every day. An owner who calls and gets voicemail - then calls another broker who picks up - is a relationship that never started. The agent makes sure the whole LAAA Team is always available, not just the ones who happen to be free.

03 How It Works

Answers every call. Routes only the right ones.

The agent handles the full inbound call flow — from first answer through qualification and handoff. Every specialist on the LAAA Team only picks up the phone for people who are ready to transact.

01

Answers immediately - every time, 24/7

Every inbound call to the LAAA line is answered within one ring, day or night. No voicemail. No hold music. A natural, conversational AI that sounds like a sharp member of your team - not a robot, not an IVR menu. The caller is greeted by name once identified, or professionally by the team name on first contact.

No missed calls
02

Qualifies the caller in under two minutes

The agent determines who is calling and why - owner looking to sell, investor seeking inventory, general market inquiry, or existing client. It asks naturally: what property are you calling about, what's your timeline, have you had a valuation done? The answers route the conversation appropriately without feeling like an interrogation.

Qualification gate
03

Handles every call that doesn't need a specialist

General market questions, cap rate inquiries, neighborhood overviews, basic valuation curiosity — the agent handles all of it completely and professionally. These callers get a genuinely useful conversation. No specialist's time gets touched. The agent closes each one with a natural invitation to start a real valuation conversation when the time is right.

Protects your time
04

Routes to the right specialist - automatically

When a caller signals seller or buyer intent, the agent captures the property address, submarket, building size, and timeline - then routes to the right LAAA specialist based on geography and deal type. A Koreatown owner goes to the Koreatown specialist. A West LA investor goes to the West LA lead. The right person gets the lead with full context - not a cold voicemail to decode.

Smart routing across the team
05

Books valuation appointments directly

For owners who are ready to move, the agent schedules a valuation call or in-person meeting directly into the team calendar - no back-and-forth, no scheduling emails. The appointment arrives confirmed, with the property address and owner's situation already summarized.

Calendar integration

Trained on your markets and deals.

The agent isn't generic. It's loaded with LAAA-specific knowledge - your seven submarkets, your transaction history, your team structure, your valuation process. It speaks like someone who has been at your brokerage for years.

Knowledge Base 02

LAAA transaction history

455+ closed deals, $1.45 billion in volume - the agent can reference the team's track record credibly when a caller asks why they should work with LAAA over another broker.

Knowledge Base 03

Valuation process and methodology

How LAAA approaches property valuations, what data is used, how long it takes, and what the owner can expect from the process — all answered accurately without any specialist having to explain it on every call.

Knowledge Base 04

Team structure and routing logic

Who handles what - Glen, Filip, and the eight specialists. The agent routes correctly based on property type, submarket, and deal size so every caller reaches the right person.

Knowledge Base 05

Buyer and investor qualification

When investors call about active listings or portfolio opportunities, the agent qualifies financing status, investment criteria, and timeline - filtering unqualified buyers before they reach the team.

Knowledge Base 06

Common objections and market questions

"Is now a good time to sell?" "What are cap rates doing in Koreatown?" "How does the current rate environment affect my building's value?" - all handled knowledgeably, keeping the conversation moving toward a valuation meeting.
