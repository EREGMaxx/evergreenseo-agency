04 The Issues, In Detail

Five findings, ranked by impact.

The site isn't broken — it's incomplete. Each of the following represents a gap between the depth of expertise LAAA actually has and what Google can read and rank.

01

Homepage is 771 words — too thin to compete

The primary ranking page for every money keyword is the homepage. At 771 words, it doesn't give Google enough signal to understand the depth of LAAA's expertise or geographic coverage. The Glaser Group's homepage is three times that length, with detailed content across submarkets, transaction types, and investor personas. Thin homepages don't rank in competitive CRE markets — this is the single most urgent fix.

02

Zero rankings on transactional keywords despite elite credentials

LAAA has the deal volume, the team size, and the submarket coverage to dominate searches like "multifamily broker Los Angeles" and "sell apartment building Los Angeles." The credentials exist on the site — they're just not structured in a way Google reads as authoritative for these specific queries. This is a content architecture problem, not a credibility problem.

03

No content for investors at the research phase

Owners considering selling an apartment building spend weeks researching before they call anyone. Queries like "how to sell apartment building in Los Angeles," "1031 exchange multifamily LA," and "what is my apartment building worth" get real search volume — and LAAA has no content for any of them. These are the highest-intent leads in the pipeline, and they're all going to competitors or general real estate portals.

04

Template design doesn't differentiate from generic CRE sites

The current site design is clean but indistinguishable from hundreds of other real estate team sites. In a market where The Glaser Group has a custom-built brand identity built around a specific domain (lamultifamilybroker.com), LAAA's visual identity doesn't signal that this is the dominant team in LA multifamily. Design directly affects conversion rate from organic traffic — visitors who land and see a generic layout bounce faster.

05

Submarket pages aren't ranking for neighborhood-level queries

LAAA covers seven LA submarkets with dedicated pages that have real depth. But those pages aren't optimized to rank for "multifamily broker San Fernando Valley," "apartment broker West LA," or "Koreatown apartment sales." These are lower-competition, high-intent searches that a well-structured submarket page can own quickly — and LAAA already has the content infrastructure to make it happen.

05 The Opportunities

Five fronts where LAAA can win fast.

The foundation is already built. The 133 deal stories, the submarket pages, the FAQ schema — these are assets most competitors would take years to replicate. The opportunity is to structure them for search and build the content layer that converts researchers into callers.

Opportunity 01

Homepage authority rebuild

A comprehensive homepage rewrite targeting the three or four highest-volume money keywords — with submarket coverage, transaction type breakdowns, and social proof woven in. This alone moves the needle on the terms that matter most.

Opportunity 02

Submarket ranking pages

Seven markets, each optimized for "[neighborhood] multifamily broker" queries. LAAA already has the content — it needs SEO architecture. This creates seven independent ranking opportunities in lower-competition local searches.

Opportunity 03

Investor education content

"How to sell apartment building in LA," "1031 exchange guide," "what is my building worth" — these queries attract owners at the exact moment they're deciding to sell. LAAA's deal experience makes them uniquely positioned to own this content cluster.

Opportunity 04

Deal stories as SEO assets

133 closed deal stories are an extraordinary trust signal — and largely invisible to search. Restructuring these pages with proper markup, internal linking, and keyword targeting turns an existing asset into a ranking engine.

Opportunity 05

Free valuation CTA

The site already offers free valuations at (818) 212-2808. A dedicated, SEO-optimized landing page targeting "apartment building valuation Los Angeles" creates a direct pipeline from organic search to inbound calls.

Opportunity 06

Link building from CRE authority

LAAA's transaction volume and M&M affiliation open doors to editorial coverage in CRE publications, local business press, and investor newsletters. A structured outreach campaign builds the domain authority that sustains long-term rankings.
