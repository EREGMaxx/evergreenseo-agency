04 The Issues, In Detail

Ten findings, ranked by impact.

Each of the following is independently fixable. Together, they are why the brand's search visibility doesn't match its real-world reputation.

01

The splash page is an SEO disaster

killershrimp.com loads with nothing but four "Click Here" links. Zero keyword content, zero crawlable text. The actual content lives at /home/ — which Google treats as a secondary page, not the homepage. This single decision undoes most of the site's other SEO work.

02

Homepage title tag wasted

The current tag reads "Home – Killer Shrimp – Restaurant and Bar." The word "Home" sits in the most valuable SEO real estate the site owns. It should read: "Killer Shrimp | Seafood Restaurant & Waterfront Dining | Marina del Rey."

03

Four fragmented domains

killershrimp.com, killershrimpcafe.com, killershrimpyachtclub.com, and killeroceanadventures.com each accumulate their own backlinks and authority signals — none of which compound. This is the single largest structural problem on the audit.

04

The menu is a PDF

/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/KS-Menu-Summer-2025-1.pdf is functionally invisible to Google. Every dish, every protein, every cocktail — every search opportunity — is locked behind a file that the search index cannot read meaningfully.

05

No schema markup detected

No LocalBusiness, Restaurant, or Menu schema was found in the page source. Competitors with schema earn enhanced rich results — stars, hours, and price range visible directly in the search listing. Killer Shrimp earns nothing. (May be JS-injected; we'll confirm at onboarding.)

06

H1 is not optimized

The current H1 reads "Welcome to Killer Shrimp." No location, no keyword, no relevance signal — just a greeting. Search engines read this as the page's main subject heading.

07

NAP discrepancy across four sources

Website and visitmdr.com list (310) 578-2250. Yelp, TripAdvisor, Discover LA, and Restaurantji list (310) 578-2293. Google reads inconsistent NAP data as a trust penalty and directly suppresses local rankings.

08

GBP claim status & activity unverified

The profile is live and rated four stars, but owner-claimed status, last post date, photo count, and which phone number is publishing cannot be confirmed without a logged-in session. The NAP discrepancy almost certainly extends to the GBP as well.

09

GAYOT.com signals "Restaurant Closed"

GAYOT.com has Killer Shrimp tagged as "THIS RESTAURANT IS CLOSED." Third-party site, but Google crawls it. Likely contributing to local ranking suppression — needs to be disputed and corrected.

10

No content strategy in place

Zero pages targeting "live music Marina del Rey," "private event venue Marina del Rey," "happy hour Marina del Rey," or "waterfront dining LA." These are high-intent clusters Killer Shrimp could own outright given its domain age and brand recognition.

Opportunity 02

Happy hour runs 3–6 PM daily. A dedicated, optimized page paired with Google Business Profile posts can own "happy hour Marina del Rey" consistently within one quarter.

Opportunity 03

Opportunity 04

Bringing all four sub-brands under a single domain concentrates authority and compounds backlinks. A sensitive conversation, but the long-term gain is enormous.

Opportunity 06

Low effort, high leverage. File a dispute directly with GAYOT and monitor local signal shift — measurable improvement typically within 30 days.
